What is Healthcare Data Management & its challenges?

Healthcare data management (HDM) or healthcare information management (HIM) is the systematic arrangement of health (medical) data in digital form. We can think of this data process as the lifecycle of health data; Data is created, stored, organized, processed, archived, and destroyed.

Data security is a major concern here, data is securely stored and protected to maintain strict confidentiality and integrity and is available only to those who require access. As health data management advances, more organizations will be collecting multiple health-related data and combining them to generate new insights that can improve patient health.

Everything from electronic medical records (EMR) and electronic health records (EHR) resulting from doctor visits to handwritten medical notes scanned into a digital repository can be called HDM, or Digital Healthcare Data Management (DHDM).

But this efficient healthcare data management possesses some challenges also; Here we list out some of them:

Fragmented Piece of Data

We can store the data in different formats like spreadsheets or databases, images or video files, digital documents, and scanned paper documents, or may be stored in specialized formats.

Data is widely duplicated, collected multiple times, and stored in different versions by healthcare providers, public health organizations, and patients themselves. Information about patient well-being has no single source of truth.

Changes in Stored Data

Medical data is constantly changing, such as the names, occupations, locations, and conditions of patients and doctors. Patients undergo many tests and receive many types of treatments over the years, and treatments and medications evolve on their own. New types of medical treatments, such as telehealth models, generate new types of data.

Regulations and Compliance of Data

Medical data is sensitive and must comply with government regulations. Data discovery challenges and poor data quality make it very difficult to conduct required audits, meet regulatory requirements, and limit the variety of data healthcare providers can use to benefit patients.

What are the Benefits of Healthcare Data Management?

The benefits of healthcare data management can be looked at from a primary perspective. Then using blockchain technology for them creates a great opportunity. The better data you have, the better decisions you can make and the better outcomes for patient healthcare. Besides the critical aspect of providing health care to help patients.

Comprehensive Health Data Records: Create a comprehensive view of patients, families, and patient groups. Integrated profiles that provide status and enable forecasts. Using it to predict patient health can enable better treatment and a better proactive approach to overall healthcare delivery.

Better Patient Engagement: Based on predictive modeling, targeted patients are provided with reminders and care instructions that may be relevant to them

Efficient Health Outcomes: Track health trends in certain regions or among specific populations, predict new trends, and suggest proactive measures to counter rising health problems.

Effective Business Decision-Making: Help healthcare providers make better data-driven decisions, such as which types of medical professionals to recruit, which equipment to invest in, or which types of patients to focus marketing efforts on.

Analyze Physician Activities: Analyze medical practitioner records such as success rates, time invested in different treatments and medical decisions, and align physicians with the goals of the healthcare firm.
