What is a Fractionalized NFT?

To put it simply, a fractional NFT is an NFT that has been divided into smaller fractions, allowing multiple parties to claim ownership of various portions of the same NFT. The fractional tokens, which can be traded or exchanged on secondary markets, will grant each token holder a portion of ownership in NFT.

How do fractionalized NFTs work?

Large digital artworks or collections of artwork can be divided into smaller parts or pieces using fractionalized NFTs. Then, various owners can receive different portions of these fractionalized pieces. This makes it possible for anyone to acquire a portion of ownership in a work or collection that would ordinarily be too costly or substantial for one person to buy outright.

Tokenizing the artwork into multiple pieces, each of which represents a portion of the original asset, is how the fractionalization process operates. These items are supported by blockchain technology and are stored on the Ethereum network. Every time a piece is purchased or sold, it is verified and tracked using secure smart contracts, which assure that its ownership status is accurate and up-to-date. and it is also distributed fairly among owners.

The Benefits of Fractionalized NFTs

One of the most appealing aspects of NFTs is that they allow you to own unique items. However, the element of exclusive nft fractional ownership is also one of the primary reasons for their ever-increasing prices. Fractional NFTs are intended to address this issue while also providing other significant benefits, such as:

Democratization

Rising NFT prices have driven many prospective buyers away from the market. The fractionalization of an NFT allows a broader range of interested buyers to gain access to it, allowing them to purchase shares of their favorite NFTs as an investment or as a gift to others.

Price determination

It is difficult to determine the correct price for an NFT, especially when no transaction history is available. After fractionalizing an NFT, its components are made available for bidding by multiple investors, which helps in determining the best price based on market demand.

Greater liquidity

Due to their high price, expensive NFTs frequently remain on the market for a longer period of time before being sold. The fractionalization process divides such NFTs into tiny, affordable fractions that can be purchased on secondary markets by a large number of investors. It lowers the risks of NFT investment, and even expensive NFTs tend to sell out quickly.

Better for creators

While all of the preceding points are about the buyer's experience, NFT fractionalization is also extremely beneficial to creators because it allows them to gain greater exposure in the highly liquid F-NFT market.

Fractionalizonal NFTs are an excellent way to gain exposure to the NFT market. Let's look at some industry-level use cases of fractionalized NFTs to get a better understanding of their real-world applications.

1. Gameplay using fractionalized NFTs

Unlike other types of crypto games, play-to-earn games allow players to earn, buy, sell, and even gift in-game digital assets. The vast majority of these assets are non-fungible tokens. Multiplayer games with NFTs, such as Star Atlas and Axie Infinity, use this technology to allow players to band together and purchase more expensive items.

2. NFTs with fractionalized metaverse

NFTs have already simplified the acquisition of virtual real estate and other digital assets in the metaverse. Furthermore, since non-fungible tokens are based on smart contracts, they simplify ownership management by handling payment distributions, royalties, and the transfer of legal ownership rights. Interestingly, with the advent of fractionalized NFTs, purchasing and selling digital assets within the metaverse has become even easier. Users can now buy and sell NFTs that have been divided into several pieces, each representing a portion of an asset.

3. Real estate and fractionalized NFTs

Real estate investments frequently generate large returns in a shorter amount of time. However, in this day and age, purchasing any property requires a significant amount of upfront capital, which means that only a small number of people can invest in the real estate sector. This is a problem that NFT fractionalization seeks to address by selling fractional shares of real estate NFTs, making real estate investment more affordable.

4. Fractionalized NFTs and the art space

Popular artists' works are frequently sold at high cost. Artists who are not well-known, on the other hand, struggle to make a living selling their art. NFTs have played a significant role in assisting such artists in making a living by selling exclusive rights to their creations. The good news is that the situation is improving with the advent of fractionalized NFTs. Even small investors can now invest in expensive art, and creators can reap significant rewards when their works are sold.

5. Fractionalized NFTs in music

Musicians are discovering hidden value in the NFT space by using fractionalized NFTs. This is a win-win situation for both the artists and their fans. Many artists have begun selling royalty shares to their fans, giving each of them the right to a percentage of all royalties from future album sales.
