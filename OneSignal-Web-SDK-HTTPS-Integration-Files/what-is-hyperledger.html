What is Hyperledger?

If you are thorough with mining and blockchain, you must have heard of a very common and frequently used term that is Hyperledger. But while cryptocurrencies like bitcoin are easy to understand, the hyper ledger is altogether a different initiative you will find in the industrial blockchain applications.

As per its official website, Hyperledger is explained as, “ an open-source collaborative effort created to advance cross-industry blockchain technologies. It is a global collaboration, hosted by The Linux Foundation, including leaders in finance, banking, Internet of Things, supply chains, manufacturing, and Technology.”

Hyperledger does not support any cryptocurrency. According to the website, “ it has a technology promised broader and more fundamental revolution than blockchain technology.” Blockchains has the potential to “build a new generation of transactional applications that establishes trust, accountability, and transparency at their core while streamlining business processes and legal constraints.”

Hyperledger is aimed to provide a platform for software developing companies to meet and create better blockchain frameworks. Founded in 2015 by the Linux Foundation, Hyperledger is a community of more than 100 members today. Well-known industry leaders and mobility tech giants like IBM, Fujitsu, SAP, Huawei, Nokia, Intel and Samsung, financial institutions like Deutsche Börse, American Express, J.P. Morgan, BBVA, BNP Paribas and Well Fargo, as well as Blockchain startups like Blockstream, Netki, Lykke, Factom, bloq and Consensys, are a part of this wide community.

As awareness increases, consumers demand omnichannel shopping and every other data related to it but retailers face problems due to improper data management in providing them.

Hyperledger is inclusive of more than 10 executives who are equipped with decades of experience and unmatched professional skills in Open Source and tight connections to several industries. The community has engineers from IBM along with the leaders of the Apache Foundation.

By assembling the great industry leaders and experiences giants all under one roof, Hyperledger provides them with an immense amount of opportunities to gain more technical knowledge and have a conversation with one another that further helps them in developing more contacts to industries and developers.

Dealing amidst the best software developers and cryptocurrency professionals, Brian Behlendorf, Executive Director denied building its own cryptocurrency. He was quoted saying that “You’ll never see a Hyperledger coin,” he added, “By not pushing a currency, we avoid so many political challenges of having to maintain a globally consistent currency.”

They firmly stand by their words and this has helped Hyperledger to be different from the rest. The company is adamant about providing consistent help to build industrial applications of blockchain technology and maintaining the open-source distributed ledger framework. Hyperledger is currently serving to promote the blockchain technologies, framework, and interfaces of the following projects:

  1. Hyperledger Sawtooth which is developed by Intel.
  2. Hyperledger Iroha which is a project of Japanese companies
  3. Hyperledger Fabric which is lead by IBM
  4. Hyperledger Burrow that develops contract along with the specification of Ethereum.
  5. Hyperledger Composer is a tool used for building blockchain business networks.
  6. Hyperledger Explorer, hosted by the parent company The Linux Foundation. It is a web application designed to view, invoke, deploy or query blocks, transactions and associated data, network information (name, status, list of nodes), chain codes and transaction families, as well as any other relevant information stored in the ledger.
  7. Hyperledger Indy collects tools, libraries and further components for digital identities that are rooted in blockchains.
  8. Hyperledger Cello works as a blockchain-as-a-service deployment model.
